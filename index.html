The Kodály Method

Everyone has to be taken to a little garden of music. Zoltán Kodály developed an effective technique to learn sight-reading, which constitutes the basis of music education in Hungary.

Using this time-proven method, musicians learn to become proficient in their understanding of music theory, and virtuosos in their daily practice.

Who I Am

My interest in music started at age 7 in Nyíregyháza.

It was my privilege to study under the guidance of some of the best and most demanding teachers.

I spent many years touring the world with the Pro Musica Choir under the direction of Dénes Szabó, and earned a degree in choir conduction, music theory, singing and piano from the Liszt Ferenz Zeneművészeti Főiskola (Franz Liszt Academy of Music).

I have been teaching in the United Kingdom for the past five years, and am a fully certified GTC teacher of music.

My Work

For the past five years, I have been teaching music to people of all ages, and take pride in their accomplishments as they reach their goals or sucessfully pass their grade examinations.

Most of my students are children who desire to learn music to further their academic possibilities; I do everything to ensure they progress smoothly and comfortably. Adults who need a vocal coach, who always dreamed of learning music or need to rehearse for a big audition, all benefit from my thorough experience and knowledge.

For smooth progress, I require my students to be assiduous in their practice (yes, there is "homework"!). And since continuity makes for efficient learning, I guarantee a lesson a week and strive to arrange make-up lessons.
